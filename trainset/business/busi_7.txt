Stock indexes falling despite strong retail data

Stocks were flat to modestly lower Wednesday after the Dow Jones industrial average is wavering in the face of posting nine straight days of gains. It would be the first time since 1999 the benchmark index has been on such a winning streak.

Investors were cautious despite a retail-sales report for February that came in much stronger than expected. Other indexes were slightly higher.

Consumers boosted spending by a seasonally adjusted 1.1% last month and posted a 0.4% gain excluding gasoline and cars, the government said.

In after trading, the Dow, the Standard & Poor's 500 and the Nasdaq composite index were slightly lower.

On Tuesday, the Dow edged higher to gain for an eighth straight day. The Dow closed at 14,450.06. The S&P 500 fell 0.2% to close at 1,552.48. The Nasdaq composite fell 0.3% to 3,242.32.

In Asia, Japan's Nikkei 225 index fell 0.61% to 12,239.66 on Wednesday. In Europe, the Stoxx Europe 600 index traded in and around the flat line.

Benchmark oil for April delivery was up 21 cents to $92.75 per barrel in electronic trading on the New York Mercantile Exchange. The contract rose 48 cents to close at $92.54 per barrel on the Nymex on Tuesday.
